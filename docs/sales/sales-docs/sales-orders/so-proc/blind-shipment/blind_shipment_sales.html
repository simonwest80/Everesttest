Blind Shipment

Blind ship is the process of shipping goods to your customer’s customer without providing details of the person actually shipping the goods (i.e., you).

Details of the actual vendor are not printed on the packing slips (in the blind ship format), sent with the goods.

A customer (A) who buys goods from you is also a retail vendor to customer (B). If (A) does not have sufficient stocks to fulfill the sales orders of (B), (A) can request you to deliver the goods as a blind shipment. You will ship the goods to (B) with either the address or logo of (A) printed on the packing slips. When (B) receives the goods, this packing slip is acknowledged. You can raise a normal sales invoice on (A) and attach the acknowledged copy of the packing slip as proof of delivery.

For a blind shipment, you have to create a normal sales invoice.

The packing slip (in blind ship format) should display the address and the logo taken from the profile of the customer (path: Invoicing > Customers > Options > In New Window > Addresses/Logo tab) on whose behalf you are sending the goods (A in the above example). To do this, select the customer on whose behalf you are sending the goods in the Customer field of the sales document.

For the packing slip to appear in the blind ship format, select the Blind Ship Format check box in the General tab of the Document Printing Preferences dialog box (path: Sales Document profile > Options > Print > Order/Invoice).
