Transaction History Information

The History tab in a sales document displays the entire history of a transaction beginning from the inception (sales quote), to the payment on the final document.

This ** tab is displayed only when you save the sales document.

The History tab displays the following information:

  • Details of sales quote creation such as date and time of creation, amount of document and the user who created the quote.
  • Details of sales order creation, such as date and time of creation, amount of document and the user who created the order. The profit made on all the items on the document is displayed if items are committed on the order.
  • Details of sales invoice creation such as date and time of creation, amount of document, user who created the invoice, and the profit made on all the items on the document.
  • Details of payments or deposits received and refunded on the document such as the payment type and reference number. Details of payments that are applied to the document are also displayed.
  • If payment is received or refunded through a credit card, the masked card number, address verification details, batch processing details and the authorization responses are also displayed. Credit card numbers are masked using the format XXXXXXXXXXXX when the **Enable Extended Mode** check box is cleared (path: **File > Setup > Preferences > Workstation Settings > Company Settings** tab).
  • Details of modifications made to documents such as changing the status of the sales order, changes in the customer contact and shipping addresses, change in the document alias, payment terms, delivery method, location/sub-location and jurisdiction.

You can export the data in the History tab to an Excel, HTML or text file.

You can set the following preferences in the Documents dialog box (File > Setup > Preferences > Flow Control > Documents > Miscellaneous > Others > Others 1 tab):

  • Maintain transaction history for documents
  • Copy transaction history attached to an order to its corresponding final document (when converting from an order to a final document)

Documents Dialog Box

To open a document, select Open Document from the right-click menu in the History tab. Options common to all browsers are also available from the right-click menu.
