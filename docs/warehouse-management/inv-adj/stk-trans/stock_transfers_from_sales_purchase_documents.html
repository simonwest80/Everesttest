Stock Transfer from Sales and Purchase Documents

Purchase and sales documents in Everest serve a dual purpose. They are not only used to record the steps in the purchase or sales cycles but also used for managing inventory movements.

When you take items into stock, you can record their acquisition in a purchase receipt.

The items received from a vendor for approval can be committed or attached to a purchase order without affecting inventory value. The movement of items that are reserved for shipment against a sales order to a packing area can be recorded against that sales order. When the items are shipped, the items are moved out from the current stock area through a sales invoice.

Advantages of using sales and purchase documents to record inventory movements are:

  • No discrepancy between Financial Ledger and Stock Ledger - A Stock Ledger is an account of the item quantities while a Financial Ledger is a record of the item value. Many accounting systems first record the entry of items in the Stock Ledger through a Goods Received Note. The purchases are accounted later when the vendor’s bill is received. The time lag between the receipt of items and the accounting results in discrepancy between the value of stocks as per the Stock Ledger and value of stocks entered in the Financial Ledger. Everest ensures that item receipts are simultaneously taken into stock and accounted for.
  • No duplication of information - When items are requisitioned from stocks, the reason for their movement has to be recorded.

If an item is shipped to a customer, the sales document against which it was sold has to be recorded.

When sales and purchase documents are used to record stock movements, this information need not be duplicated.

  • Easy retrieval of information - Everest integrates inventory management with other business processes. This makes for easy retrieval of information from a single source.
