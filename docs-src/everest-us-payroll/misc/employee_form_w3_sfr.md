Check this box if you file Form 941, Employer’s Quarterly Federal Tax  Return, and no other category applies to you except Third-party sick pay.


![]({{site.prl_baseurl}}/img/example.gif) A church  or church organization should check this box even if it is not required  to file Form 941.

Check this box if you are a military employer who sends Forms W2 for  members of the uniformed services.

Check this box if you are an agricultural employer who files Form 943,  Employer’s Annual Federal Tax Return for Agricultural Employees, and who  also sends Form W2 for agricultural employees. You can send the Form W2  for non-agricultural employees with a separate Form W3 by selecting the  appropriate check box.

Check this box if you want to file Form 944, Employer’s Annual Federal  Tax Return, and no other category applies except Third-party sick pay.

Check this box if you are a railroad employer sending form W2 for employees  covered under the Railroad Retirement Tax Act (RRTA).  Do not show employee RRTA  tax in boxes 3 through 7. These boxes are only for social security and  medicare information. Send the Form W2 of employees who are subject to  Social Security and Medicare with a separate Form W3; also, check the  box 941 on that Form W3.

Check this box if you are a household employer sending Form W2 for household  employees; also you have not included household employees’ taxes on Form  941 or Form 943.

Check this box if you are a US, State, or local agency filing Form W2  for employees who are only subject to the 1.45% medicare tax.

Check this box if you are a third-party sick pay payer (or are reporting  sick pay payments made by a third party) filing Form W2 on which the "Third-party  sick pay" option in box 13 is selected. You must also check another  box such as 941 if you select the Third-party sick pay check box.


File a single Form W3 for all the Form W2s that you have filed for regular  and third-party sick pay Form W2.

# Employee Form W3

: This is an optional box that you may use for numbering  the whole transmittal.
: Enter the control number of the W3 form generated  for a year. Make certain that entries do not cross over into the form  identification number box (33333).

: Check the appropriate box that applies to your company.  Check only one box unless the second, checked box is “Third-party sick  pay”. If you have more than one type of Form W2, send each type (except  Third-party sick pay) with a separate Form W3.

**c) Total Number of Forms W-2**
: Total number of completed Form W2s for the selected  year is displayed by default. Voided Forms W2 are not included in this  list. You cannot modify this field.


**d) Establishment Number**
: Enter your establishment number, which is used to  identify separate establishments of your business. You may file a separate  Form W3 with Form W2s for each establishment even if they have the same  EIN number, or you may file a single Form W3 for all Form W2s of the same  type.


**e) Employer Identification Number**
: The **Federal ID** number entered in the **Payroll 
 Company Setup** profile is displayed by default. Verify that the  Employer Identification Number is correct. This number should be the same  as the one shown on your Form 941, Form 943 and Form CT-1 in the following  format: 00-0000000.


**f) Employer’s Name**
: The company name specified in the **Payroll 
 Company Setup** profile is displayed by default. This name should  be the same as the one shown on your Form 941, Form 943 and Form CT-1.


**g) Employer’s Address and ZIP Code**
: Employer’s address and the zip code specified in  the **Payroll Company Setup** profile  are displayed by default below the **Employer’s 
 name** field.


**h) Other EIN used this year**
: If you have used an Employer Identification Number  (EIN) (including a prior owner’s EIN) on forms submitted to the IRS that  is different from the EIN entered in **Box 
 e**, enter  that number used.
: Enter the following information for use by the SSA  if any questions arise during processing: contact person, telephone number,  fax number, and e-mail  address.


**1 Wages, Tips, Other Compensation**
: The total amount taxable for wages and tips for  the selected year is displayed.


**2 Federal Income Tax Withheld**
: This field displays the total amount of income tax  withheld from the employees’ pay by adding up the amounts specified in  each Form W2s for the selected year.


**3 Social Security Wages**
: This field displays the total amount taxable for  social security by adding up the amounts specified in each Form W2 for  the selected year.


**4 Social Security Tax Withheld**
: This field displays the total amount of social security  tax withheld from the employees’ pay by adding up the amounts specified  in each Form W2 for the selected year.


**5 Medicare Wages and Tips**
: This field displays the total amount taxable for  Medicare wages and tips by adding up the amounts specified in each Form  W2 for the selected year.


**6 Medicare Tax Withheld**
: This field displays the total amount of Medicare  tax withheld from the employees’ pay by adding up the amounts specified  in each Form W2 for the selected year.


**7 Social Security Tips**
: This field displays the total amount taxable for  social security tips by adding up the amounts specified in each Form W2  for the selected year.


**8 Allocated Tips**
: This field displays the total amount taxable for  allocated tips by adding up the amounts specified in each Form W2 for  the selected year.


**9 Advance EIC Payment**
: This field displays the total of advance EIC payments  made to the employees by adding up the amounts specified in each Form  W2 for the selected year.


**10 Dependent Care Benefits**
: This field displays the total amount taxable for  dependent care benefits by adding up the amounts specified in each Form  W2 for the selected year.


**11 Nonqualified  Plans**
: This field displays the total amount spent on non-qualified  plans by adding up the amounts specified in Box 11 in each Form W2 for  the selected year.


**12 Deferred Compensation**
: The total of the amounts specified with codes D–H,  S, Y, AA and BB in box 12 of all the Form W2 created for the selected  year is displayed.


**13 For third-party sick pay use only**
: Third party payers of sick pay (or employers filing  W2 with the amount of sick pay using the optional rule for Form W2 as  described in section 6 of Pub 15 A) who are filing [Third-party  Sick Pay Recap Forms W2 and W3]({{site.prl_baseurl}}/misc/third-party_sick_pay_recap_forms_w2_and_w3.html) need to enter 'Third-party Sick Pay  Recap" in this box.


**14 Income tax withheld by payer of third-party  sick pay**
: Enter the total income tax amount withheld by the  payer of third-payer sick pay.
: Complete this box only if you are the employer and  have employees who had income tax withheld on third-party payments of  sick pay. Show the total income tax withheld by third-party payers on  payments to all of your employees. Although this tax is included in the  box 2 total, it must be separately shown here.


**15 State**
: Displays the state abbreviation for the employer’s  address. The ID number of the state is displayed in the adjacent field.


**Boxes 16–19**
: These fields display the total of state/local wages  and income tax shown in their corresponding boxes on the Form W-2 included  with this Form W-3. Verify that the amount reported in each box is an  accurate total of Form W2s.


**Employer’s State I.D.  No.**
: The **State ID**  number that you specified in the **State 
 Setup** profile is displayed by default.


**Contact Person**
: Enter the contact person’s name in this field.


**E-mail  Address**
: Enter the contact person’s e-mail  address.


**Telephone Number**
: Enter the contact person’s telephone number.


**Fax Number**
: Enter the contact person’s fax number.


**Form W3 Year**
: Specify the year for which you are creating the  **Form W3** report.


**Preview**
: Use this option for a print preview.


**Print**
: Use this option to print the form.


{:.note}
If you try to print the **FORM 
 W3**, **Everest Payroll** checks  if the total of Box 3 + Box 7 lies within the upper social security limit  for the year. If this total exceeds the limit, the value to be printed  in Box 3 will be equal to the upper social security limit minus the value  in Box 7.


{:.warn}
You cannot submit a copy of Form W3 that is  printed on plain paper to IRS or SSA.  You are required to use only pre-printed stationery for the purpose of  submission.
