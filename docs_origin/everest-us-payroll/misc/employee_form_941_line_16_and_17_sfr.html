On February 6, 2007, Pine Co. discovered that it had over reported the social security tax on a prior quarter return by $2,500. Its Form 941 taxes for the first quarter of 2007 were as shown below:


 January $2000
February $2000
March $2000



Pine Co. adjusts liabilities on line 15 as follows to offset the over reported amount of $2500:


 Month 1 $2000
Month 2 $0.00
Month 3 $1500
Total $3500



Since the error was not discovered until February, it does not affect the liabilities reported in the month of January, that is, Month 1 of line 15. For the month of  February, the entire amount of $2000 is used to offset the over reported tax liability; thus, Month 2 of line 15 displays zero. The balance amount of $500 is adjusted against tax for the month of March; hence, $1500 makes up the amount for Month 3 on line 15.


Using the above example, if the over reported social security tax on a prior quarter return was $10,000, Pine Co. could carry the excess negative adjustment of $6,000 ($10,000 - $2,000 - $2,000) to the next quarter. Pine Co. would only report $4,000 of the adjustment on line 7e because line 10 must equal the total shown in line 15. Part 4 - Details

This section allows you to designate an employee, a paid tax preparer, or another person to discuss your Form 941 with the IRS.

By doing so, you entitle IRS to contact the designee for any information on your tax returns.

You also authorize your designee to do the following:

  • Provide any information that is missing from your return
  • Gather information about processing your return
  • Respond to certain IRS notices that you have shared with your designee about math errors and return preparation. IRS will not send notices to your designee.

To expand your designee’s authorization, see Pub. 947, Practice Before the IRS and Power of Attorney.

The authorization automatically expires one year from the due date for filing your Form 941. If you or your designee want to terminate the authorization, write to the IRS office for your locality using the “Without a payment” address under Where Should You File on page 4.
