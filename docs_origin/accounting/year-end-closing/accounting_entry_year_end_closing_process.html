Accounting Entries - Year end Closing Process

When a year is closed, accounting entries are created in a system general journal. The first entry transfers the difference between the income and expense accounts to the current earnings account. This entry is created with the description "Transfer to Retained Earnings account".
Account Debit/Credit
All Expense Accounts (each account is listed) Credit
All Revenue Accounts (each account is listed) Debit
Retained Earnings Account Debit (in case of loss)


Credit (in case of profit)
The second entry closes the balance sheet accounts and is created with the description "Closing balance carried forward to next year".
Account Debit/Credit
All Asset Accounts (each account is listed) Credit
All Liability Accounts (each account is listed) Debit
Retained Earnings Account Debit/Credit (the net balance)
These accounting entries are created as the last entry in the year being closed, irrespective of when the actual year closing process is carried out. A third entry is created as the first entry of the following fiscal year stating the opening balances of the balance sheet accounts. The description for this entry is "Opening balances brought forward from previous year".
Account Debit/Credit
All Asset Accounts (each account is listed) Debit
All Liability Accounts (each account is listed) Credit
Retained Earnings Account Debit/Credit (the net balance)

The accounting entries created during the year end closing process are automatically posted. You can view these entries by selecting Options > Posted Journals from the Unposted General Journals browser (path: Accounting > General Journals).
