Generate Serial Numbers

You can generate serial numbers (path: Credit Order profile > Options > Attach/Retrieve Serial # > Generate Serial #) for items that you want to bring into your stock from credit orders and credit memos. The serial numbers generated are attached to the credit order/memo. You can also generate serial numbers for negative quantities in sales invoices.

This feature has the following uses:

  • If you have received returns from customers for items that were sold before you migrated to Everest, you can use the Generate Serial # option to track these items.
  • You can use this option to account for services provided or to keep track of the return of serialized items that you have not sold. You can then assign the manufacturer’s serial numbers, if any, and bring the items back into stock.

You are an authorized service center of a computer vendor. Customers come into your center to service items that they have bought in any other store or to return defectives. You then return this item to the computer vendor. In such a case, generating new serial numbers helps you keep track of such items.

If you select this option from the Credit Order profile, the serial numbers are transferred to an ‘Out’ type of stock area.

Committed Quantities - Stock Area Assigned

If you select this option from the Credit Memo profile, the serial numbers are transferred to a ‘Stock’ type of stock area.

Returned Items - Stock Areas Assigned

To generate a serial number from a credit order profile, do the following:

  • From the Credit Order profile, select Options > Attach/RetrieveSerial # > Generate Serial #. You will see the Generate Serial # browser.
  • Enter the serial number to be generated in the Gen. Quantity column and click OK.

You can also generate serial numbers in a credit memo.
