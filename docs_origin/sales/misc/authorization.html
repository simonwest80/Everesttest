Authorization

An authorization is a request to charge a cardholder. When a charge is authorized, the cardholder’s available limit reduces, but the funds are not actually captured. The card is not billed until a request is made by the merchant to capture the transaction.

Your customer, who has a credit limit of $10,000, places an order for goods worth $4000. The available limit reduces to $6000 although the customer is not required to pay the $4000 until you send a request with the authorization code for capturing the funds. The funds are then transferred to your account and the customer’s account is debited.
