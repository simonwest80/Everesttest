Inventory Adjustments

Inventory adjustments are the adjustments made to the book values of items in stock in terms of cost and quantity to reflect the actual values or quantities of the stock.

Inventory adjustments are of three types:

  • Cost adjustments - Where adjustments are made to the cost of an item in stock.
  • Price protection - Where adjustments are made to stock either to correct errors in recording purchase costs at the time of receipt or invoicing, or to account for supplemental vendor bills.
  • Quantity adjustments - Where adjustments are made to the quantity in stock. The quantity may be either reduced or increased.
